Life - How I choose to live it

“The important thing is to not skip questioning. Curiosity has its own reason for existing.”

– Albert Einstein

As far as back I can remember, my dad has always found my inquisitive nature to be inspiring. Frequently asking me why and how questions about subject with which I found a passionate curiosity positively affected my growth and development. After 23 years, even though I am half a globe away from home, I maintain constant communication with my father who continually pushes me to seek out and discuss the why’s and how’s of everyday life. Being an avid reader of ‘The Economist’ and having deep interest in the opportunities for digital change in our society, my strongest interests align within the field of Artificial Intelligence and Computer Vision with a focus on education for underprivileged.

I am constantly envisioning the blend between technology, business, design and innovation. More importantly blending these four areas with education. Having experience working with Maa Foundation during summer of 2015, I realized that there are millions of talented individuals who are deprived of opportunities. Working with children whose parents think that education does not matter was eye opener for me. My ultimate goal is to develop affordable and accessible education for these children and open up opprtunities for them to excel.

I have been taking online classes through edX since its launch. Learning is a continuous process and I am extremely fortunate to have access to resources to further my knowledge in various domains. My ultimate goal in life is to create a difference in the education sector in India, promote creative and innovative learning environment to children. Develop tools and means to not just educate children but also enhance teaching capabilities of teachers and reform the entire learning experience.

In 2015, I decided to further my knowledge in Business domain which started by taking HBX CORe classes by Harvard Business School. It taught me the fundamentals of Business in areas of Business Analytics, Economics for Managers and Financial Accounting. Later, I took Managing Your Career that taught me various opportunities and career paths for leaders. It was an eye opener because it widened my horizons. Following up on that, I became a fan of disruption and innovation. What could be better than taking Disruptive Strategy course from the legend Professor Clayton Christensen? Hence, in 2017, I registered for the course and I have been a fan ever since.

My boat has set sail, are you with me?
